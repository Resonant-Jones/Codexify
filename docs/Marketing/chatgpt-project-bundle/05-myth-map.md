# Method 2: Hijack the Myth

People adopt new tools through myths already installed in their imagination.
Codexify should attach to durable myths rather than inventing a myth from scratch.

## Myth Map

| Myth | Existing emotional meaning | Codexify expression | Safe marketing language | Claim risks |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sovereign builder | Agency, ownership, self-direction | Own your AI workspace boundary and memory posture | Build on infrastructure you can inspect and govern | Overclaiming autonomy or full self-management |
| Cyberdeck operator | Tactical competence, technical control | Operator-visible runtime truth and workflow control | Operate AI with explicit runtime surfaces | Suggesting unsupported operations maturity |
| Private library | Durable personal canon | Conversations and docs become retrievable project intelligence | Turn scattered notes into usable intelligence | Implying perfect recall or zero loss |
| Workshop | Iteration, craft, refinement | Threads, artifacts, and revisions accumulate into working knowledge | Refine ideas inside one continuity surface | Claiming automatic quality outcomes |
| Observatory | Seeing systems clearly | Health, catalog, events, and proofs make state visible | Inspect what the system knows and does | Implying complete observability across all paths |
| Second brain | Externalized cognition | Durable continuity layer for projects and workflows | Keep your thread of thought across time | Overpromising cognitive replacement |
| Indie hacker | Leverage under constraints | Local-first stack for builder pragmatism | Ship with owned infrastructure, not rented context | Overstating turnkey simplicity |
| Steward of knowledge | Responsibility and care | Claim discipline, provenance, and truth-bound updates | Grow intelligence without losing accountability | Overstating governance/compliance guarantees |

## Usage Rule

Pick one primary myth per campaign. Do not mix more than two myths in one short-form asset.
