# Method 3: Open the Hidden Door

Codexify's hidden door is AI systems literacy.
The product becomes more valuable as users learn the language and standards of trustworthy AI operations.

## Literacy Topics to Teach

- retrieval boundaries
- runtime truth
- provenance
- memory policy
- identity modeling boundaries
- workflow encoding
- task acceptance versus completion
- local-first deployment posture
- claim discipline
- operator observability

## Core Teaching Angles

- Why AI chat history is not the same as memory
- What happens after you press send?
- Accepted does not mean completed, and why that matters
- How to turn documents into retrievable project knowledge
- Why user-owned AI needs truth surfaces
- From scattered prompts to an intelligence layer

## Content Formats

- explainer posts
- diagrams
- trust stories
- tutorials
- philosophy notes
- case studies

## Execution Rule

Every literacy asset should:
1. introduce one concept,
2. show one observable surface,
3. close with one practical action.
