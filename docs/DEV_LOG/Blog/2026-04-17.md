# Daily Log — April 17, 2026

This was a day of tightening the shell around places where it had been too permissive. The first move was Flow Builder: it stopped assuming the surrounding shell would stay in view and got its own way back to Guardian. That sounds small until you look at the failure mode it removes. A route that can strand the user is not just awkward; it is a broken boundary. The new behavior keeps the prior Guardian path reachable even after the draft fields take focus, which turns Flow Builder from a detour into a bounded lane.

After that, the work moved into the composer, where width pressure had been making the control surface less legible than it should be. The send button was realigned, then the whole rail was hardened so the controls do not reshuffle themselves when the viewport gets tight. The important part is not the visual polish. It is that the interaction order now stays stable as the space compresses. That keeps the user's mental map intact, which is what a serious composer surface should do.

Persona Studio got the final pass, and that one was about render truth. The shell now presents itself as a clearer two-lane structure with a session-local ephemeral harness and a subordinate utility pane for profiles and diagnostics. A new render-safety test makes the mount contract explicit: the edited shell, truth matrix, utility pane, and ephemeral harness all have to appear together. That does not equal live proof, and it should not be mistaken for it. What it does is reduce the odds of a future refactor quietly dropping part of the surface and only revealing the damage in manual use.

The deeper shift across the day is that three different parts of Codexify stopped relying on incidental layout behavior. Flow Builder now has a credible exit. The composer now keeps its control order when the width collapses. Persona Studio now has a stronger structural contract around what it mounts. That is the kind of work that does not look dramatic in isolation, but it lowers drift and makes the system more trustworthy in the places users feel immediately.
